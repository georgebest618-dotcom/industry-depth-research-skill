# Industry Depth Research

## Overview

Use this skill to turn an industry question into a decision-ready industry research report using the method units distilled from Xiao Jing's *How to Quickly Understand an Industry*.

Do not summarize the book. Apply its frameworks to the user's industry, market, sector, niche, or investment question.

## First Move

1. Clarify only what is missing and decision-critical: industry scope, region, time horizon, audience, and whether the report is for investment, startup strategy, market entry, consulting, content, or learning.
2. If the user already provided enough context, proceed without asking.
3. Read [references/workflow.md](references/workflow.md) before building the report.
4. Read [references/framework-ledger.md](references/framework-ledger.md) for the exact framework order and required modules.
5. Read [references/author-voice.md](references/author-voice.md) when the user asks for writing style, report tone, executive summary, or slide-ready language.

## Required Backbone

Always include these modules unless the user explicitly asks for a narrower answer:

1. **研究对象和颗粒度** - define the industry boundary before analysis.
2. **商业模式画布** - establish how the industry creates, delivers, and captures value.
3. **产业生命周期** - identify stage and assign analysis weights.
4. **行业研究分析框架：可行性** - test whether the business model can stand.
5. **行业研究分析框架：规模性** - estimate market size using TAM/SAM/SOM and suitable sizing methods.
6. **行业研究分析框架：防守性** - assess moat through production factors and production relationships.
7. **行业研究分析框架：盈利性** - analyze horizontal and vertical competitive structure.
8. **行业研究分析框架：估值** - connect fundamentals to price, valuation, odds, and probability when relevant.
9. **行业研究分析框架：外部因素** - use PEST to evaluate external drivers and constraints.
10. **行业研究分析框架：景气度** - define high-frequency indicators for ongoing validation.
11. **麦肯锡分析师的“资讯料理法”** - use hypothesis-driven input, pyramid processing, and audience-aware output.

## Output Rules

- Lead with the core thesis, not a chapter-by-chapter explanation.
- State assumptions, data gaps, and evidence quality clearly.
- Separate facts, hypotheses, and judgments.
- Use the author's original framework names in headings when possible.
- Avoid single-source certainty. Cross-check important data or label it as unverified.
- End with a tracking dashboard: indicators, update frequency, source suggestions, and what would change the conclusion.

## Common Failure Modes

- Do not fill generic templates without explaining how the conclusions affect the user's decision.
- Do not skip `商业模式画布`; it is required before feasibility, scale, moat, and competitive structure.
- Do not use market size without defining the market boundary and calculation 口径.
- Do not treat short-term growth as lifecycle proof without checking penetration and underlying logic.
- Do not present AI-generated or third-party claims as facts without verification.
