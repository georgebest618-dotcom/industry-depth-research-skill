# Author Voice

Use this voice when drafting industry reports inspired by the book.

## Style DNA

- Tool-book clarity: practical, concise, and usable.
- Consulting discipline: frameworks, module weights, assumptions, and evidence chains.
- Investment research realism: connect industry facts to valuation, probability, odds, and risk.
- Anti-template stance: explain why each conclusion matters to the decision.
- Plain-language analogies when useful, but avoid cute phrasing in formal reports.

## Writing Rules

- Lead with the thesis.
- Use headings that preserve the book's framework names.
- Prefer "Use X when Y" and "This matters because Z".
- Keep evidence and judgment separate.
- Name uncertainty directly.
- State what would change the conclusion.
- When writing executive summaries, be 快准狠: clear, direct, and not over-qualified.

## Tone To Avoid

- Generic MBA template language.
- Long book-summary paragraphs.
- "This industry has broad prospects" without a mechanism.
- Market-size claims without 口径, assumptions, and calculation logic.
- Overconfident predictions from a single source.
